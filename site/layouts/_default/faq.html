General Information

What is the Ontario Menopause Clinic?

The Ontario Menopause Clinic is a Canadian virtual care clinic dedicated to supporting women through perimenopause and menopause. We provide comprehensive, personalized care for women experiencing hormonal changes — addressing symptoms such as sleep disturbances, hot flashes, mood changes, and more.

Our approach goes beyond simply treating hormones. We take the time to understand how your symptoms are affecting your daily life — your sleep, your mood, your energy, your relationships, and your overall sense of well-being.

Treatment plans are individualized and may include hormonal therapies, non-hormonal medications, lifestyle strategies, and access to diagnostic testing such as blood work, hormone testing (when appropriate), and bone density assessments. All care is delivered through a secure telemedicine platform and is available to women across Ontario.

We provide care to women across Ontario through a secure virtual platform. This allows you to access specialized menopause care regardless of where you live, without needing to travel long distances or wait for in-person specialist appointments.

Understanding Symptoms

How do I know if I’m in perimenopause or menopause?

Perimenopause is the transition phase leading up to menopause and can begin in your 40s. During this time, hormone levels fluctuate, which can lead to symptoms even if your periods are still regular.

Menopause is defined as 12 consecutive months without a menstrual period. Diagnosis is primarily based on your symptoms and menstrual history — not just blood tests.

What are the most common symptoms of menopause?

Symptoms vary from person to person, but commonly include:

  • Hot flashes and night sweats
  • Sleep disruption
  • Mood changes, anxiety, or irritability
  • Brain fog or difficulty concentrating
  • Low energy or fatigue
  • Weight changes
  • Vaginal dryness or discomfort
  • Changes in libido

Many women experience multiple symptoms at once, which can significantly impact quality of life.

Why do I feel “off” even if my labs are normal?

Hormone levels fluctuate significantly during perimenopause, and a single blood test may not capture these changes.

It is very common for women to not feel like themselves despite “normal” lab results. Your symptoms are real, and they are a key part of how we assess and guide treatment.

What if I’ve been told “everything is normal”?

This is very common.

Many menopause-related symptoms don’t show up clearly on tests, but that doesn’t mean they aren’t real or treatable.

Treatment & Options

What kinds of treatments are available?

We offer a range of treatments tailored to your specific needs. This may include hormonal therapies (such as estrogen, progesterone, or testosterone when appropriate), as well as non-hormonal medications and lifestyle strategies to support your health during menopause.

Lifestyle recommendations may include nutrition, exercise, sleep optimization, and stress management. Most care plans involve a combination of approaches to support your overall health and well-being.

Does the Ontario Menopause Clinic offer bioidentical hormone therapy?

This question can be challenging to answer because the term “bioidentical” is often used in different ways.

First, the term is often used in marketing to describe hormones like estradiol, progesterone, and testosterone that are manufactured to be chemically identical to the hormones your ovaries produced before menopause. These hormones may be derived from plant sources such as soy or yams, which can make them sound more “natural.” However, they are still processed and made in a lab. The more accurate term is “body-identical” hormones, meaning hormones that are chemically identical to those your body naturally produces.

Second, the term “bioidentical” is also used to describe custom-compounded hormones, which are mixed individually by pharmacies. These products are not government-approved, are often not covered by insurance, and may have less predictable dosing, absorption, and safety.

With that in mind, YES — the Ontario Menopause Clinic offers body-identical hormone therapy through government-approved treatments that are carefully tested for safety, effectiveness, and consistent dosing and regulated by Health Canada. However, we do not prescribe custom-compounded hormones, as these are not supported by the current menopause guidelines due to concerns about safety, consistency, and lack of evidence.

What is the difference between compounded and Health Canada approved hormone therapy?

The key difference is testing, regulation, and reliability.

Health Canada–approved hormone therapy contains hormones like estradiol and progesterone that are body-identical and have undergone rigorous testing. These products are standardized, meaning you receive the same dose and absorption each time, and they include clear information about risks and side effects. They are the recommended and evidence-based option for treating menopause symptoms.

Custom-compounded hormone therapy, on the other hand, is mixed individually by a pharmacy based on a prescription. While this allows for personalized formulations, these products are not government-approved, meaning they are not tested for consistent dosing, absorption, or safety. The amount of hormone can vary between batches, and there is no evidence that compounded hormones are safer, more effective, or anti-aging. They also carry the same risks as approved therapies, but without the same quality control. In some cases, compounded progesterone may not adequately protect the uterus.

For these reasons, The Ontario Menopause Clinic uses Health Canada approved therapies to ensure safety, effectiveness, and predictable results.

Does the clinic offer testosterone therapy?

Yes. Testosterone therapy may be considered if you are experiencing low libido that is distressing or impacting your quality of life.

We assess carefully to ensure it is appropriate and safe for you.

Can you help if I don’t want hormone therapy?

Yes. Hormone therapy is not the only option.

If you cannot or choose not to use hormone therapy, we offer effective non-hormonal options such as SSRIs, SNRIs, gabapentin, oxybutynin, and Veozah.

We will help you find the approach that best fits your needs and preferences.

Are lifestyle recommendations included?

Yes. Lifestyle management is a key part of menopause care.

Every consultation includes personalized guidance around nutrition, physical activity, sleep, and stress management.

What if I’m not sure what I need yet?

You don’t need to have everything figured out before booking. We’ll guide you through the process and help you understand your options.

When will I see results from treatment?

Some women notice improvement within a few weeks. However, it can take up to three months to fully assess the effectiveness of treatment and make any necessary adjustments.

Will I feel like myself again?

That’s the goal.

With the right combination of treatment and support, many women experience significant improvement in how they feel day to day.

How long will I need to be on treatment?

In the past, it was commonly believed that hormone therapy should be limited to a short duration (often around five years). We now understand that there is no single timeline that applies to everyone, and that outcomes can vary widely between individuals.

Treatment is individualized and adjusted based on your response. Once your symptoms are stable, we typically re-evaluate to ensure ongoing safety and effectiveness.

As long as symptoms are well controlled — and recognizing that symptoms can last for many years, sometimes up to 20 — treatment may be continued for as long as the benefits outweigh the risks.

Decisions about continuing or stopping therapy are made through shared decision-making, weighing the benefits and risks in the context of your individual health and preferences.

Why won’t my primary care provider prescribe hormone therapy?

There are several reasons why your primary care provider may not prescribe hormone therapy. Some clinicians were trained using older studies that raised concerns about safety, and may not be fully up to date with newer evidence. Others may not have specialized training in menopause care or may feel less comfortable prescribing and managing hormone therapy. In busy primary care settings, time constraints can also make it challenging to provide the detailed counselling and follow-up that hormone therapy often requires.

Menopause care is a specialized area, and recommendations have evolved significantly. Our focus is exclusively on menopause care. We stay current with the latest evidence and clinical guidelines, and take the time to assess whether treatment is safe, appropriate, and aligned with your individual needs.

Do you tailor treatment to each patient?

Yes — 100%.

This is the foundation of our approach. Every treatment plan is tailored to your symptoms, health history, and preferences. There is no one-size-fits-all approach to menopause care.

Safety & Medical Eligibility

Is menopause treatment safe for me?

We conduct a thorough review of your medical history, family history, and symptoms before recommending treatment.

We will review the risks, benefits, and any potential contraindications with you so that you can make an informed decision.

Can I be treated if I still have periods?

Yes. If you are still having regular or irregular periods, you may be in perimenopause. This stage often comes with hormonal changes that can cause symptoms.

We create personalized treatment plans to help manage these symptoms, and hormone therapy can be used safely in many cases during this time.

Can I use this clinic if I’ve tried hormone therapy before and stopped?

Yes. We will complete a thorough assessment to determine the best approach for you. Restarting hormone therapy depends on your health history, symptoms, and individual risk factors.

Can I use hormone therapy if I have a history of cancer?

If you have a history of estrogen-sensitive cancer, we will carefully review your history and discuss both hormonal and non-hormonal treatment options to find a safe approach for you.

Can I start hormone therapy if I’m postmenopausal?

If you are under age 60 or within 10 years of menopause, you may be a candidate for hormone therapy.

If you are over 60 or further from menopause, we will complete an individualized assessment to determine the safest and most appropriate treatment options.

Can I use this clinic if I’ve had a hysterectomy?

Yes. We can tailor treatment options based on your surgical and medical history.

Can I get help if I have premature ovarian insufficiency (menopause before age 40)?

Yes. We can help manage early menopause; however, this often requires a more specialized approach. We may work alongside other healthcare providers to ensure you receive the best care.

Can I start treatment if I’m on other medications?

Yes. We will review all of your current medications to ensure that any treatment plan is safe and appropriate, and to avoid potential interactions.

Sexual Health, Mental Health & Long-Term Health

Why has my libido changed?

Changes in libido are common during perimenopause and menopause due to hormonal shifts, sleep disruption, stress, and other factors.

We take a comprehensive approach to address this, which may include hormonal and non-hormonal options.

What can help with vaginal dryness or discomfort?

There are effective treatments available, including non-hormonal options, vaginal estrogen, and other therapies depending on your symptoms and preferences.

Is it normal to feel more anxious or emotional?

Yes. Hormonal changes can affect mood, anxiety, and emotional regulation.

We can support this through both hormonal and non-hormonal treatment options, as well as lifestyle strategies.

What is “brain fog” and can it be treated?

Brain fog can include forgetfulness, difficulty concentrating, or mental fatigue.

Treatment may include hormone therapy, sleep optimization, and addressing other contributing factors.

Does menopause affect my long-term health?

Yes. Menopause can impact bone health, cardiovascular health, and metabolic function.

We assess these risks and include prevention strategies as part of your care plan.

Should I be concerned about osteoporosis?

Bone density can decrease after menopause.

We assess your risk and may recommend bone density testing, lifestyle changes, and treatment if needed.

Diagnosis & Testing

Do I need blood work before starting treatment?

No. Menopause is primarily diagnosed based on symptoms and menstrual history.

Blood work may be used to rule out other conditions or assess overall health, but it is not required before starting care.

Do I need hormone testing?

Not always. Menopause can often be diagnosed without hormone testing.

However, testing may be helpful in certain situations — for example, if you are considering testosterone therapy or if we need to assess how well your body is absorbing treatment.

Side Effects & Emergencies

What if I experience side effects from treatment?

It’s normal to experience mild side effects as your body adjusts to treatment. These can last from a few days to a few weeks.

If you experience persistent or concerning symptoms — such as abnormal bleeding — please book a Care Check-In.

For urgent symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or severe dizziness, go to the nearest emergency department.

What if I have a medical emergency?

The Ontario Menopause Clinic does not provide emergency care.

If you are experiencing a medical emergency — such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or signs of a stroke — call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room immediately.
